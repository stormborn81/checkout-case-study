Diagnosing a 21pp Checkout Conversion Decline

An end-to-end product analytics exercise: metric specification, metric tree decomposition, and root cause analysis for a fictional e-commerce platform.

Built as part of the AI Analyst for Builders course. All analysis performed on the NovaMart synthetic e-commerce dataset (50K users, 6.5M events, 47K orders) using Claude Code as an AI analytical partner.

Part A

The Metric

Before investigating a decline, you need a bulletproof definition. The metric spec took 5 iterations to get right.

Checkout Conversion Rate

62.6%
COUNT(DISTINCT users with completed order within 30d) / COUNT(DISTINCT users with checkout_started) × 100
Unit Per user (deduplicated)
Window 30 days from checkout start
Numerator 27,206 users with completed orders
Denominator 43,490 users who started checkout
💡
Why this matters: The first draft used session-based counting, which produced 33% instead of 63%. A user who tries checkout 3 times and succeeds once is a success story, not a 33% converter. Getting the unit of analysis wrong changes the entire narrative.

Part B

The Decline

Monthly checkout conversion dropped from 68.1% to 30.0% over 2024. But the headline hides a more nuanced story.

Jan-May: Sharp decline as user base grows rapidly. Early months had a small, high-intent cohort.
Jun-Oct: Stabilization around 30-33%. The "steady state" emerges.
Nov: Black Friday spike to 34.3% (weekly peak: 52.3%).
Dec: Drop to 30.0% despite a Holiday Sale running all month.

Where in the funnel?

The decline concentrates in the first two steps. Order completion stayed flat.

Root Cause Analysis

Observation

What exactly happened?

+

Checkout conversion declined from 69.2% (Q1) to 46.3% (Q4), a 22.9pp drop. Monthly cohort rates fell from 68.1% (Jan) to 30.0% (Dec).

November showed a temporary spike driven by a single Black Friday week (52.3%), which did not repeat during the December Holiday Sale despite similar promotional mechanics.

Decomposition

Break the metric into its components

+

Two composition effects drive the decline, not a broken funnel:

1

New vs Returning Mix Shift

Returning users grew from 0% to 71% of checkout starters. They convert at a stable ~29% vs ~50% for new users. As the lower-converting group grows, the blended rate falls mechanically.

Hypotheses

Seven testable hypotheses across four cause categories

+
H1 Mix Shift Confirmed

Growing share of returning users (lower-converting) dilutes the blended rate mechanically

H2 Mix Shift Confirmed

Early-adopter effect: Jan cohort was small and high-intent, inflating the starting baseline

H3 Technical Partial

Mobile checkout degradation: mobile converts lower than web (44% vs 59%)

H4 External Confirmed

Promotional cannibalization: Holiday Sale discounts purchases that would have happened anyway

H5 External Plausible

Christmas shipping anxiety: Holiday Sale runs Dec 15-31 when delivery cutoffs close, with no express option and no delivery date visibility

H6 Product Rejected

Checkout form is the constraint: redesign experiment showed zero lift

H7 Product Rejected

Deferral features help: save-for-later experiment actually decreased conversion by 3.2pp

4

Testing

The Black Friday vs Holiday Sale puzzle

+

The most interesting finding: two similar promotions produced completely different results.

Black Friday

+22pp lift
Discount 25%
Duration 7 days
Returning user lift +24pp (25% → 49%)
Pull-forward 10.8% bought again in Dec

Holiday Sale

0pp lift
Discount 20%
Duration 17 days
Returning user lift 0pp (stayed at ~29%)
Promo usage 100% of Dec orders used it
🔍
The insight: 100% promo usage with 0% conversion lift means the Holiday Sale is discounting purchases that would have happened anyway. The discount timing (Dec 15-31, when shipping cutoffs close and Christmas anxiety peaks) may actively work against conversion rather than for it.

-3.2pp conversion. Giving users an easy way to defer reduces purchase urgency.

5

Findings

The funnel is not broken. The story is composition.

+
1

The decline is a composition effect

Growing share of returning users (0% to 71%) and TikTok channel mix shift mechanically lower the blended rate. Individual segment rates are stable or improving. This is normal for a growing platform.

2

Returning users CAN convert at high rates

They hit 49% during Black Friday (vs baseline 29%). The ceiling is structural but not permanent. Urgency interventions work; deferral features backfire.

3

The checkout form is not the constraint

The redesign experiment proved this. Two of three metric tree L2 levels have tracking gaps. We cannot diagnose further without instrumenting intermediate checkout events.

4

The Holiday Sale is pure waste

100% promo usage + 0% conversion lift = discounting purchases that would have happened anyway. Restructure or eliminate.

Recommendations

What To Do Next

This Sprint

Instrument the checkout funnel

Add address_entered, shipping_selected, payment_info_entered events. Without them, every checkout improvement is a guess.

Unlocks targeted fixes

Add estimated delivery date to checkout

Only one shipping tier ($5.99 standard), no delivery visibility. Tests the shipping anxiety hypothesis directly.

Builds purchase confidence

Run checkout exit survey for returning users

The 29% ceiling is structural (flat across devices, channels, attempt count). We don't know WHY these users start checkout without buying.

Identifies the actual barrier
Next Quarter

Evaluate TikTok on revenue per acquired user

Lower conversion may be offset by lower CPA. Benchmark separately from organic/paid search.

Redesign the Holiday Sale

Start earlier in December (Dec 1-14) to align with shipping windows, or offer free/reduced express shipping with guaranteed pre-Christmas delivery instead of a product discount.

Test urgency interventions for returning users

Limited-time pricing, low-stock indicators. Black Friday proves they convert at 49% under urgency. Avoid deferral features.

Do NOT

Redesign the checkout form again

The experiment proved the form is not the constraint.

Add save/wishlist features

The save-for-later experiment showed these reduce conversion.

Panic about the year-long decline

It is a composition effect from user base growth, not a broken funnel.

Methodology

Dataset

NovaMart synthetic e-commerce dataset: 50,000 users, 6.5M events, 47,199 orders across 2024. 12 tables including experiments, support tickets, and promotions.

Tools

Claude Code as AI analytical partner. DuckDB for querying. Python (matplotlib) for visualization. Notion for documentation.

Framework

Metric Anatomy Template for specification. 5-step root cause framework: Observation, Decomposition, Hypotheses, Testing, Findings. Dual-threshold health monitoring.

Collaboration
